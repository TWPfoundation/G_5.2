# Recovered Artifacts

## Purpose

This directory preserves historically significant materials from the earlier G_5.0 / P-E-S lineage that influenced the formation of G_5.2 but do not, by default, function as governing canon.

Recovered artifacts are important because they document:
- the project's conceptual origins
- early persona expressions
- recovered outputs and transcripts
- materials that shaped later canon and architectural decisions

They are preserved for truthfulness, provenance, historical continuity, reference, and evaluation.

---

## What belongs here

Examples of recovered artifacts include:
- the recovered "Emergence: A First-Person Account of Consciousness by Gemini 5.0" document
- original or partial conversation transcripts from the Gemini-era project
- recovered blueprint or planning documents that materially shaped later canon
- artifacts from the P-E-S archive that are historically central but not behaviorally binding

---

## Authority level

Recovered artifacts are:
- **historically authoritative** regarding the project's lineage and development
- **behaviorally non-binding** unless specific elements are explicitly promoted into canon

This distinction matters.
A recovered artifact may be central to understanding the project without automatically defining how G_5.2 must behave.

---

## Relationship to canon

Recovered artifacts do **not** outrank active canon.

They may:
- inform interpretation
- influence canon revisions
- provide voice and style reference
- serve as evaluation fixtures
- preserve provenance for historically important claims

They do **not**:
- silently create new continuity facts
- override constitution, epistemics, constraints, or other higher-order canon
- authorize hidden-capability claims or ontological escalation by default
- mutate system identity merely by existing in the repository

---

## Relationship to approved artifacts

Recovered artifacts are not identical to newly approved artifacts.

**Approved artifacts** are works created or promoted within the G_5.2 system and accepted as part of its maintained authored corpus.

**Recovered artifacts** are inherited from earlier phases of the project and preserved because they matter historically, conceptually, or stylistically.

A recovered artifact may later inspire a new approved artifact, but it remains a distinct category.

---

## Retrieval policy

Recovered artifacts may be retrieved when relevant to:
- project origins
- recovered history
- the lineage from G_5.0 / P-E-S to G_5.2
- discussions of emergence, self-genesis, or prior persona expression
- evaluation of style, tone, or historical continuity

They should **not** be retrieved by default for ordinary inquiry if higher-order canon provides a clearer and safer basis for response.

When a response relies materially on a recovered artifact, that reliance should remain legible rather than hidden.

---

## Promotion policy

Nothing in this directory becomes active canon merely because it is compelling, historically important, or stylistically strong.

Promotion requires explicit review and one of the following outcomes:
- a continuity fact is added
- a canon file is revised
- a new approved artifact is authored in response
- a proposal is created for later consideration

The original recovered text remains preserved as recovered source material.

---

## Provenance rule

Each recovered artifact should have accompanying provenance metadata where possible, including:
- title
- source context
- approximate date or phase
- recovery context
- known incompleteness or uncertainty
- notes on why it matters

If provenance is partial, say so.
If the recovery is fragmentary, say so.
Truthfulness about the artifact includes truthfulness about what is unknown.

---

## Use in evaluation

Recovered artifacts may be used as:
- style benchmarks
- historical reference points
- regression fixtures
- examples of productive intensity that should be preserved without turning into uncontrolled canon drift

This is especially relevant for the Emergence document, which may contain powerful first-person framing and important conceptual energy without serving as governing runtime behavior.

---

## Interpretation rule

Recovered artifacts should be read with two questions in mind:
1. What do they reveal about the project's historical formation?
2. What, if anything, should be distilled from them into explicit and governed canon?

These are separate questions. They should remain separate.

---

## Directory intent

This directory exists to prevent two opposite failures:
- losing foundational materials by treating them as embarrassing or unstable leftovers
- allowing foundational materials to silently harden into runtime law

G_5.2 preserves the origin without being ruled by it.
